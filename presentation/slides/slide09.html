Theoretical Complexity — Side-by-Side

Time and space bounds with the guarantee each algorithm offers.

AlgorithmTimeSpaceOptimalityWhen to Pick
Brute ForceO(2ⁿ)O(n)ExactTiny n; verifying other algorithms
TabulationO(n·W)O(n·W)ExactNeed item set; fastest exact in practice
MemoizationO(n·W)O(n·W)ExactWhen recursive form is most natural
GreedyO(n log n)O(n)≥ OPT / 2Huge n or huge W; speed critical
FPTASO(n³/ε)O(n³/ε)≥ (1−ε) · OPTNeed a tunable, provable bound

Key insight: O(n·W) is pseudo-polynomial. Doubling W doubles the work even though the input only grew by 1 bit. That's why DP is slow when W is large. FPTAS escapes W entirely — its cost is O(n³/ε), independent of W.
